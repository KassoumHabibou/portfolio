Our team **Blue Bird** finished **4th worldwide** 🏆 in the [AI for Trade Global Challenge](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7426625935988264961/), an international competition organized by the **Center for Collective Learning (CCL)** that asked participants to forecast U.S. and China trade flows for October 2025.

We designed **SABLE** (*Seasonal Anchor with Boosted Lagged Exogenous*), a machine learning model that combines a seasonal anchor with gradient-boosted lagged exogenous features to predict bilateral trade flows. What makes this result special: none of us had a background in trade economics — we treated the challenge as an opportunity to learn a new field from scratch, and it paid off.

This was a true team effort with my co-contributors [Bachir Sabo](https://www.linkedin.com/in/bachir-sabo-087b7a247/) (Data Scientist, The World Bank), [Cheikh Fall](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7426625935988264961/) (Finalyse) and [Khalil-Jonathan Jewiti-Rigondza](https://www.linkedin.com/in/khalil-jonathan-j-9b69a0174/) (Biostatistician, Amaris Consulting). Many thanks to the organizers at the Center for Collective Learning, Veronika Hamar and Luis Felipe López-Calva. The full code and methodology are available in the repository linked above.
