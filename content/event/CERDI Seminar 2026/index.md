summary: 'Economic Inclusion and Intimate Partner Violence in Mauritania (joint work with Julia Vaillant and Kelsey A. Wright, World Bank Gender Innovation Lab)'
abstract: "This presentation examines how Mauritania's Tekavoul social safety net program and complementary economic inclusion interventions affect intimate partner violence (IPV) among vulnerable women in rural areas. The research compares outcomes across cash grants, psychosocial support, combined interventions, and a control group. A key finding is that psychosocial components appear to mitigate the potential adverse effects of women's economic empowerment initiatives in fragile contexts. Joint work with Julia Vaillant and Kelsey A. Wright (World Bank, Africa Gender Innovation Lab), as part of the Sahel Adaptive Social Protection Program (SASPP)."
